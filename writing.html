Comma Errors


Ethics/Morals Essay

Morals nearly mirror ethics but morals are internal.
Morals nearly mirror ethics, but morals are internal.
The first sentence was wrong because the “but” was trying to conjoin two independent clauses without a comma.

Ethics/Morals Essay

Morals change similar to ethics when an event causes a reaction by an individual, changing their beliefs.
Morals change, similar to ethics, when an event causes a reaction by an individual, changing their beliefs.
The phrase “similar to ethics” is extra information and should be separated by two commas, forming an interjection, to not confuse the reader.

Ethics/Morals Essay

Next tier down Organizations/Social Groups also impact a person’s internal views.
Next tier down, Organizations/Social Groups also impact a person’s internal views.
The clause “Next tier down” is a fragment or incomplete thought, and therefore must be separated by a comma.

Hamlet In-Class Essay

However, with a broader lense, Claudius’ quote also pertains to all of his conversations with other characters.
However with a broader lense, Claudius’ quote also pertains to all of his conversations with other characters.
Having a comma after however creates a choppy rhythm to the sentence, confusing readers.

Hamlet In-Class Essay

After failures to obtain his revenge on Claudius Hamlet vows during the final Acts for his “thoughts be bloody, or be nothing worth!” (Shakespeare IV.iv.66).
After failures to obtain his revenge on Claudius, Hamlet vows during the final Acts for his “thoughts be bloody, or be nothing worth!” (Shakespeare IV.iv.66).
The first clause is a fragment and needs to be connected to a completed clause with a comma.

Hamlet In-Class Essay

Tiring of failing in his sworn duty, Hamlet fully commits himself to vengeance, and isolates himself from the other characters, by not conforming with the societal norm of seeking power.
Tiring of failing in his sworn duty, Hamlet fully commits himself to vengeance, and isolates himself from the other characters; not conforming with the societal norm of seeking power.
The comma before “by” makes no sense here. To fix the sentence, I added a semicolon to eliminate the run-on sentence and show a connection between two clauses.

Beginning of the Year Example:


"'It excited him, too, that many men had already loved Daisy--it increased her value in his eye' (Fitzgerald 149). The words “It excited him”represent Gatsby’s first reaction to Daisy. She excites him because she could grant him access to the status and wealth he wants. Just as he wants fame and fortune, Gatsby only wants Daisy. By using the pronoun “It”, Fitzgerald suggests that Daisy serves as an object to Gatsby. A means to achieving his goal of striking it rich. She had many people court her before and it made her a more elusive prize. Gatsby walks through the world of the rich; a place where he does not belong. Men expect women to serve as ever-loyal objects for their own needs during the nineteen twenties."

Not only do I not introduce the quote well, I count 3 separate ideas with this one paragraph. In addition, I do not follow my ideas through completely. I present one idea and move on to the next one without even explaining the connection to the reader.

End of the Year Example:


"Allie’s death occurs when Holden 'was only thirteen, and they were going to have me psychoanalyzed and all, because I broke all the windows in the garage' (Salinger 38-39). Breaking all the windows in a garage clearly indicates that his brothers death affects Holden in a drastic way. Holden’s violent outburst pushes 'his parents [to] consider psychoanalysis way back then, at age 13' (Dexter Martin 508). Coping with his brothers death, Holden begins idealizing all the great qualities of Allie, namely Allie’s innocence. Holden never truly reconciles with Allie’s death. The inability to move on from traumatizing situations explains Holden’s inability to accept change. Holden’s failure to move on from his brother starts his unhealthy growth as he develops into an adult."

In this example I introduce my quotes and explore on 2 solid ideas. I use the second idea to transition to the next paragraph, creating a consistent flow through my paper; also connecting it to my thesis and overarching claim..
